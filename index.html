Role Studio — per-plant roles, with flexibility kept in check

Three ideas, one order: plants carry what the company bought · people get a role per plant · review shows everything at a glance. Amber always means a deviation. Grey always means "not in this plant's plan".

The hierarchy — modules cap, tiers default, exceptions flex

We sell product modules to companies. A module is a sellable slice of the product that unlocks permissions — each permission still lives in its role type. Modules are switched on per plant, on the plant record itself: the plant carries its company label and its module flags. There is no workspace or group in between.

Effective access at a plant = user permission ∧ plant module. Module flags are written only here (Global Admin at onboarding; Technical Admin afterward) — every other screen reads them.

Plant records — company label + licensed modules
💡This table is the source of truth for what exists at each plant. Toggle a module to simulate a contract change — the configurator, the review and the previews all respect it instantly. In production only a Global Admin (at onboarding) or Technical Admin can write here.
1 · Which plants does this person touch?
💡Pick every plant they work at or view — even in different capacities. You'll give each plant its own role in step 2. Plants are grouped by the company on their record.
💡A grant adds administration on top of the role — it never changes day-to-day operating permissions, and it applies at every assigned plant. Most people need none.
2 · Give each plant its role
💡One row per plant. Same person, different plant, different role — that's normal here. Most rows should stay standard: pick the role, done. Open “Customize” only when this person needs more or less than the standard at that one plant.
3 · Review & save
💡Nothing saves until every deviation has a written reason — deviations are audited and counted as drift. Standard rows save silently.

Access review — the cross-check surface

    
One screen answers “what can this person do, at which plant, right now — and why?” Nothing here is editable — it's the reviewer's view, computed live.

    
📖How to read it: each row is an ability, each column is one plant. Click any cell and it explains itself in one sentence — who granted it, why, and what would switch it off. The colors never change meaning:

    

      ✓ comes with the role
      ✚ added by exception — reasoned & audited
      ⊘ removed by exception
      ▢ in the profile, but the plant's plan doesn't include it
      − not granted
